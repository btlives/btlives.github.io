Communities

In order to combat against knife crime we have partnered up with some communities around London to help people who have been involved in such and are seeking help to get better, those who have lost a loved one to a knife incident, those who just want more information about how they can help and trying to get youths involved in clubs so that hey are in a positive environment learning new skills, working with others and putting their knowledge to good use.

Fight for change is a sport for development charity, using sport as a way to get young people and adults together. This includes boxing, martial arts and fitness training ensuring that these people put their skills to good use to work in teams and change their outlook on life.

Fight 4 Change

South London counselling services provides free therapeutic counselling services for all.
Services includes motivational activities to black and ethnic minority, Refugees and Asylum seekers, who are living, working, training or studying in South London boroughs of Lewisham, Greenwich, Lambeth and Southwark.

South London councelling Services

Chaos theory work closely with individuals to diffuse conflict proving support for those who are in custody and community involved in high risk behaviours to create change through our prison in-reach and outreach services.

Chao Theory

Street doctors teaches others how to effectively deliver simple first aid that can save lives to those that are injured.
This includes the use of visual props and role playing to engage people more.
People who join this 3-5 month program are able to work their way up becoming a qualified first aider and even start to volunteer to teach others in the session.

Street Doctors

Unique Talent work with families, advocate, work in unrecognised areas using their experience to communicate with everyone in a unique way. They engage young people to work together to combat violence and allow these same people to have links to apprenticeships in railway, bricklaying, mechanics, construction, media, and administration, this helps young people to become independent and makes it easier for them to find work for those who are struggling.
